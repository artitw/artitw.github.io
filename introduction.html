Scenario
The Illumini Fish of Bobo Pond are hungry and waiting for you to feed them. If you feed them enough, they will award you a magical prize to express their gratitude. Don’t be too slow in feeding them, since they won’t be hungry for long, and be careful of the Evil Fisherman dwelling within the pond, who will do what he can to prevent you from satisfying the fish.

Game Sequence
  1. Insert Penny
  2. Wait for start light to turn on
  3. Adjust how fast Evil Fisherman spins
  4. Press start button to begin 60-second game sequence
  5. Game begins with Stage 1, where all six fish are hungry (eyes lit)
  6. Feed the fish with food from the tray.  Be careful of the Evil Fisherman.
  7. Points awarded for feeding the hungry fish (eyes lit).
  8. Fish eyes will dim when they are fed.
  9. Stage 2 begins after the initial six fish are fed: a random fish will become hungry.
  10. When the random fish is fed, another random fish will become hungry.
  11. By feeding at least 10 fish in the 60-second game duration, the player is awarded a gumball.

Mechatronic Penny Arcade Requirements

Conditions

  1. Must dispense SWAG to players who perform well
  2. Must be a self contained entity
  3. A team of three class members will construct each MPAG
  4. The team must construct the MPAG. It is not acceptable to simply buy significant portions of the project
  5. Game must begin with the insertion of a standard U.S. penny
  6. Must have a minimum of 3 interactions with the player
  7. At least one of the interactions should require an analog response from the player
  8. At least one of the interactions must involve non-contact sensing
  9. Game duration should be in the range of 20-60 seconds
  10. The time should be limited and a clear indication given when that time has expired.
  11. Must react to user input in at least two separate ways
  12. At least one of the reactions must be electro-mechanical
  13. At least one of the reactions must take on a variable number of levels (at least three) based on an analog input controlled by the user
  14. Reactions from the MPAG should stimulate at least two senses
  15. Clear indication should be given when a visitor has explored all of the possible actions for a given MPAG
  16. The entire operation of the MPAG must take place in a footprint no more than 3 feet wide by 2 feet deep
  17. Must be usable without human instruction

Fulfillment

  1. If player scores at least 10 points within 60 seconds, a gumball will dispense at the end of the game
  2. No other devices outside of the game are necessary to play
  3. Our team consists of Artit, Chi, Chingju (3 people)
  4. The only component already constructed was the Build-A-Bear sound board, which we spliced up and incorporated into our circuit
  5. Only after the user inserts a penny can he/she press Start to begin
  6. (1) Push the Start button to begin he game, (2) Throw balls to feed the fish, (3) Control Evilness factor
  7. Evilness factor is controlled by adjusting the knob, which sets the Evil Fisherman’s spin speed
  8. There are IR emitters and sensors that detect when a fish is fed
  9. The game time is set to 60 seconds
  10. After the game time expires, all the fish eyes will turn off and the Evil Fisherman will stop spinning
  11. (1) Detect if the player feeds the fish and updates score, (2) Adjust Evil Fisherman’s spin speed based on Evilness factor controlled by user
  12. The Evil Fisherman is controlled by a DC motor with PWM
  13. The Evil Fisherman’s Evilness (spin speed) is controlled by the Evilness knob
  14. (1) Sound: “I love you” for feeding the fish, (2) Sight: hungry fish’s eyes light up and score display on 7-segment LEDs
  15. All the actions available are clearly displayed on the front panel
  16. Our product is 3 ft wide and 2 feed deep
  17. In addition to the basic instructions, the game play is intuitive
